Certainly! The **Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC)** is a committee within the **Federal Reserve System (the Fed)** that oversees the nation's **open market operations**. These operations involve the **buying and selling of United States Treasury securities**. The FOMC consists of **12 members**, including the **seven members of the Board of Governors** and **five of the 12 Reserve Bank presidents**². Their primary responsibility is to **determine U.S. monetary policy**, which impacts interest rates, inflation, and economic growth. They meet **eight times a year** to discuss potential changes to near-term monetary policy, aiming to promote a healthy national economy¹. The committee members fall into categories: **hawks** (favoring tighter policies), **doves** (favoring stimulus), and **centrists/moderates** (somewhere in between). The current chair is **Jerome Powell**, who began his second term in May 2022, and the vice-chair is **John Williams**¹.
